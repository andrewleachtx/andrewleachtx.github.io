    OpenGL Object Shading

    We can render a 3D/2D graphic onto a static image, as seen in my rasterizer writeup. But what if we want the scene to move? What if we want to interact with the scene with our own inputs?

    Open Graphics Library, or OpenGL, is one medium allowing us to render our 2D/3D rasterized graphics, abstracting away various complexities in the rendering pipeline and hardware space.

    OpenGL, when paired with GLFW allows for us to spin up a quick state machine with C++, giving us control to render shapes, textures, and shaders in real time.
