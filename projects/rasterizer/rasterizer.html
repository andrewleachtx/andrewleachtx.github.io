    Rasterization

    Almost every 3D game or application we've played was using rasterization behind the scenes.

    If a raster is a group of pixels on a 2D grid or image, then rasterization is the process by which we convert 3D shapes (composed of triangles) into pixels on your 2D screen.

    Why compose objects of triangles though?

    Triangles Are OP

    If you wanted to estimate a 3D shape or surface as composed of a bunch of smaller polygons, you are allowed to use anything that encloses 2D area, right? In other words, as long as there are >= 3 points for the shape, you can use it.

    Naturally, the first to meet that requirement is the triangle. Then the square or "quad". However, the triangle is the simplest shape required to enclose a 2D area - which makes it perform great.

    Of course, that is to say performance is usually the goal, at least for real-time rendering in video games or other contexts. Now, if creating perfect images and scenes (prerendered) is your goal, ray tracing is your friend.
