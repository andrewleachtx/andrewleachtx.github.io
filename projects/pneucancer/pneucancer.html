    Classification of Breast Cancer & Pneumonia with Unbalanced Medical Imaging

    One domain of interest in machine learning is in medical diagnostics. Diagnosis can be an indefinite process, often dependent on having the right experts available at the right time to review data on a case-by-case basis. Even then, experts can be wrong, and may be unable to analyze the infinitesimally fine details within the data.

    If deep neural networks can be trained to observe fine details and recognize patterns in medical imaging data, accurately diagnosing medical conditions, then perhaps many lives can be saved.

    That said, much of the medical imaging data for training these networks is imbalanced - that is to say there are vastly more examples of healthy patients than unhealthy, or vice versa. The goal of this research is to develop a highly useful classification model for diagnosing medical conditions through trial performance evaluation, despite these imaging constraints.
